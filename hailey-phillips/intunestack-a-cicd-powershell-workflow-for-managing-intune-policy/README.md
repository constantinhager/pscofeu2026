# IntuneStack - a CI/CD PowerShell workflow for managing Intune policy

## Abstract

Managing Intune does not have to be any different than managing the rest of your environment, yet it often feels like it is. Drift creeps in, scoping becomes inconsistent, and mysterious exclusions quietly undermine reliability and trust.

What if Dev, Test, and Prod were not separate environments at all, but simply different deployment stages. Groups handle segmentation. Promotion is driven by success criteria defined in advance. Confidence comes from artifacts you can actually rely on.

In this session, we will walk through the foundation needed to treat Intune like the rest of your infrastructure. Using PowerShell and GitHub Actions, you will see how Intune policy can become a deployable artifact that moves predictably through Dev, Test, and Prod, with guardrails that support change instead of slowing it down.

This is about bringing pipelines, consistency, and trust to Intune without rewriting your environment or abandoning the PowerShell skills you already have.
