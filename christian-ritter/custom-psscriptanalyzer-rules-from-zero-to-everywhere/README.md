# Custom PSScriptAnalyzer Rules: From Zero to Everywhere

## Abstract

Heard of PSScriptAnalyzer before? No? Maybe you’ve noticed those mysterious squiggly lines in VS Code trying to tell you something. That’s it, your friendly neighborhood code critic. In this session, we’ll lift the curtain on how it really works.

I’ll guide you through the world of rules (yes, we’ll meet the Abstract Syntax Tree), show you how to craft your own, and weave them into your DevOps pipelines and VS Code setup. Along the way, we’ll explore practical examples from enforcing consistent naming with a snake_case rule to catching hardcoded credentials and even identifying Microsoft Graph permission oversharing using a custom rule that analyzes your script’s actual API calls.

And because great tooling doesn’t just point fingers, we’ll look at how to turn those warnings into self-healing suggestions inside VS Code, where PSScriptAnalyzer can not only highlight issues but help fix them automatically.

These rules will keep your scripts clean, your builds compliant, and your team’s bacon out of the fire.
